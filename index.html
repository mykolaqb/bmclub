A student club at the Faculty of Management that studies real companies through three lenses — company, market, and business model — and invites the practitioners behind them to speak.

What we do

We study real companies
and talk to the people behind them

Each cycle the club selects a real business — past subjects include Amazon, Hexagon AB, Strategyzer, VAMI Media, and NAC — and distributes research across three teams. Each team covers one story. Together, the three stories form one complete picture of the company.

🏢

Company Story

What the company does, how it operates, and how it grew — sourced from primary data.

🌐

Market Story

The market the company operates in, its competitors, and how it positions itself.

💡

Business Model Story

How value is created, delivered, and monetised — structured with the Business Model Canvas.

Meeting format

Work before the meeting
Share results at the meeting

The meeting is a space for presenting results. All research and preparation takes place before the session. Time at the meeting is used for presenting, asking questions, and hearing from the guest.

01

Teams prepare their stories

Each team researches and builds their part of the story before the meeting. All materials are uploaded to Miro at least 24 hours in advance.

02

Stories are presented at the meeting

Each team presents their story to the full group. Three stories combine into one picture of the company.

03

A practitioner joins as a guest

We invite a practitioner connected to the company being studied. They respond to the stories, add context from their experience, and answer questions prepared in advance by the group.

04

Afterparty

After meetings with guests, the session closes with one of the club's business games or an open discussion.

Afterparty activities

Three games we play
to sharpen your thinking

After guest meetings the club runs one of three business games. All games are played in the group — no advance preparation required.

Activity 01

Pitch Game

One person draws a startup card with a short description of a business idea. They have 3 minutes to prepare and then pitch the startup to three investors. Each investor holds a challenge card describing a specific business bottleneck. Investors present their challenges one at a time; the pitcher responds to each directly. After all challenges, each investor states a verdict — In or Out — with one sentence of reasoning.

Activity 02

Decision Room

One participant prepares a real business case in advance — a situation in which a company faced a high-stakes decision, such as the Netflix/Qwikster split in 2011. Participants are divided into teams and act as the company's executive group. Each team discusses the situation and commits to a decision. Teams present their decisions one by one. The host then reveals what the company actually decided and what the outcome was.

Activity 03

YC Partner Simulator

Each player draws a startup idea card — a short concept of 3–4 sentences. With 3 minutes of preparation, the player presents the idea as a pitch at a Y Combinator partner meeting. The pitch covers four points: the problem the startup addresses, how the product works, why this moment is relevant for the market, and what the startup is requesting from investors. After each pitch, the group votes on whether to fund and gives one round of direct feedback. The format runs on speed and precision.

Standards

How the club operates

A set of shared standards that define how the club runs:

Meetings are for sharing results — all work takes place before the meeting.
Tasks are chosen by members, not assigned.
Communication is in factual terms.
All contributions are uploaded to Miro at least 24 hours before the meeting.
Cameras are on during online meetings.
Members arrive on time and prepared.
Attendance is recorded by 10:00 on meeting day.
